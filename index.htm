Research Interest

My main research interest is on the theoretical foundations of evolutionary algorithms. I am building a geometric theory that unifies the various flavours of evolutionary algorithms across representations. Ideally, this framework aims at eliciting general fundamental principles and use them for the principled design of evolutionary algorithms for new representations and new problems with provably good performance.

 

Short Bio

I obtained a PhD degree in Computer Science from the University of Essex under the supervision of Prof. Riccardo Poli, Master and Bachelor degrees in Computer Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Turin, Italy, and I did my Master Thesis in Computer Science at the Technical University of Eindhoven, The Netherlands. Before the current appointment, I was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow affiliated with the School of Computing and with the Centre for Reasoning at the University of Kent working with Dr. Colin Johnson. Previously, I was an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Engineering at the University of Coimbra, Portugal, and member of the Evolutionary and Complex Systems Group. I also worked as a Researcher for the Hewlett-Packard Research Laboratories, UK.
